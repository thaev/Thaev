THAEV — FOUNDATION CHARTER
Project Name: THAEV
Organization Name: THAEV Foundation
Release Date: June 6, 2026
Author: The Anonymous Originator of THAEV

PREAMBLE
The THAEV Foundation is established as a non-profit organization with the sole mission of protecting, nurturing, and promoting the development of THAEV as a Common Heritage of Humanity.
The Foundation does not own THAEV. The Foundation is the trustee appointed by the community to ensure that this infrastructure forever belongs to humanity, is developed transparently, and is never captured by any force.
Who holds the keys? The community. The Foundation is merely the instrument through which the community exercises its will.
________________________________________
ARTICLE 1: NAME, DOMICILE, AND LEGAL STATUS
1.1. Name
The official name of the organization is the THAEV Foundation.
1.2. Domicile
The principal place of business of the Foundation shall be located in a jurisdiction with a favorable legal framework for non-profit organizations and open-source activities. The specific location shall be determined by the Foundation Council within the first 12 months.
1.3. Legal Status
The THAEV Foundation is an independent non-profit organization, not owned by any individual, corporation, or government.
________________________________________
ARTICLE 2: MISSION AND PRINCIPLES
2.1. Mission
The THAEV Foundation exists to:
•	Protect the integrity of the THAEV Manifesto and its core principles.
•	Nurture and support the community in developing THAEV.
•	Transparently manage public goods funding for the development of the infrastructure.
•	Protect the THAEV name and brand from misuse.
2.2. Immutable Principles
All activities of the Foundation must adhere to the core principles of THAEV:
•	Open source and absolute transparency.
•	Decentralization with no single point of control.
•	Non-profit; no pursuit of financial gain for any individual or group.
•	For humanity; serving the interests of all humankind.
________________________________________
ARTICLE 3: ORGANIZATIONAL STRUCTURE
3.1. Foundation Council
The Foundation Council is the highest governing body of the Foundation, consisting of 7 members.
3.2. Composition
•	3 members elected by the community through voting based on Badges of Radiance.
•	2 members representing the core technical development teams.
•	1 member representing the user community.
•	1 independent member, an expert in law or technology ethics, invited by the Council.
3.3. Term of Office
The term of office for each Council member is 3 years. A member may be re-elected for a maximum of two consecutive terms.
3.4. The Originator
The Anonymous Originator of THAEV shall hold the role of Permanent Honorary Chair of the Foundation. This role carries no voting rights but includes the right to offer opinions and make recommendations to the Council.
3.5. Decision-Making
Decisions of the Council are made by majority vote (at least 4 out of 7 votes in favor). Important decisions (amending the Charter, changing licenses, dissolving the Foundation) require at least 6 out of 7 votes in favor and must be ratified by the community.
________________________________________
ARTICLE 4: COMMUNITY VOTING MECHANISM
4.1. Voting Principle
All major decisions affecting the future of THAEV must be ratified by the community through voting.
4.2. Voting Rights
Voting rights are based on Badges of Radiance and Dynamic Trust Score (DTS), not on token holdings (as THAEV has no token). This mechanism ensures that those who have genuinely contributed and earned trust within the community have the greatest voice.
4.3. Voting Weight
The voting weight of each DID is calculated based on:
•	The number and quality of Badges of Radiance.
•	Overall trustworthiness (DTS).
•	The relevance of the badges to the matter being voted on.
4.4. Types of Voting
•	Technical Votes: Deciding on protocol upgrades and architectural changes.
•	Financial Votes: Approving annual budgets and major grants.
•	Governance Votes: Electing Council members and amending the Charter.
________________________________________
ARTICLE 5: GRANT PROCESS
5.1. Funding Sources
The THAEV Foundation receives funding from:
•	Voluntary contributions from the global community (in Bitcoin, stablecoins, or fiat currency).
•	Grants from public goods organizations and research foundations.
•	Retroactive Public Goods Funding programs.
5.2. Proposal Process
Any individual or group may submit a proposal to receive funding for work contributing to the THAEV ecosystem. Proposals must include:
•	A description of the work and expected outcomes.
•	The amount requested and the timeline for completion.
•	The proposer's Badges of Radiance and contribution history.
5.3. Review Process
•	Small Grants (under 1 BTC or equivalent): Reviewed by a subcommittee of the Council, with results made public.
•	Large Grants (over 1 BTC or equivalent): Require a vote by the full Council and the community.
5.4. Retroactive Mechanism
In addition to prospective grants, the Foundation implements a Retroactive Public Goods Funding (Retroactive PGF) mechanism — rewarding contributions that have proven their value over time, based on community voting.
________________________________________
ARTICLE 6: FINANCIAL TRANSPARENCY
6.1. Public Ledger
All financial transactions of the Foundation — including all receipts and expenditures — are recorded on a public ledger, auditable by anyone at any time.
6.2. Periodic Reporting
The Foundation publishes quarterly financial reports, detailing all income and expenses, with explanations for every significant expenditure.
6.3. Independent Audit
Annually, the Foundation shall engage an independent auditor to examine the books and publish the results for the community.
6.4. Administrative Cost Cap
The administrative and operational costs of the Foundation shall not exceed 10% of the total annual budget. At least 90% of resources must be devoted directly to the development and promotion of the THAEV ecosystem.
________________________________________
ARTICLE 7: BRAND PROTECTION
7.1. Trademark Ownership
The name "THAEV", the slogan "FOR HUMANITY / VÌ CON NGƯỜI", and related symbols are registered by the Foundation as assets of the Common Heritage. The Foundation holds these rights as a trustee on behalf of the community, not as an owner.
7.2. Use of the Brand
Any project or product wishing to use the THAEV name and brand must comply with the Manifesto and be approved by the Foundation Council.
________________________________________
ARTICLE 8: AMENDMENT OF THE CHARTER
This Charter may be amended if:
•	A proposal is made by at least 2 members of the Council or by the community (with a minimum threshold of supporting signatures).
•	It is approved by the Council with at least 6 out of 7 votes in favor.
•	It is ratified by the community through a vote.
Any amendment must be consistent with the THAEV Manifesto and must not weaken the non-profit, decentralized, and open-source nature of the project.
________________________________________
ARTICLE 9: DISSOLUTION
The THAEV Foundation may only be dissolved if:
•	The full Council (7/7) consents.
•	The community ratifies with an absolute majority.
•	All remaining assets of the Foundation are transferred to one or more other non-profit organizations sharing the same mission.
In any event, the source code and documentation of THAEV — already released under free licenses — remain in the public domain, and the community retains the right to fork and continue development.
________________________________________
ARTICLE 10: EFFECTIVE DATE
This Charter shall take effect on the date the THAEV Foundation is formally established. Until that time, it serves as a public commitment to how the Foundation will be governed.
